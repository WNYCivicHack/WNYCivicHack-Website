AT&T, United Way, UB ACM, InfoTech WNY, UB STOR, Buffalo Open Data and WNY Innovation Hotspot partnered together to come up with the all new WNY Civic Hack this coming fall.

This October 10 - 14, come join us to learn something new, build an app or start a company in our very own Western New York.

Winners

Grand Prize ($2,000)

Community Air Quality developed by John O'Brien (Amherst) and Kris Eckert (Hamburg); The civic app analyses air quality from a broad range of qualitative and quantitative responses to illustrate the state of the air in a specific neighborhood. It allows a user to register their phone number in the general database and answer a small set of questions on the relationship between individual lung health and environmental factors (asthma, smoking, allergies), individuals receive regularly scheduled surveys to gather information on their neighborhood set-up and related health experiences (frequency of asthma attacks). Along with the surveys, the application is equipped with additional tools that can be accessed via special keywords. These tools can be used to report sources of pollution as well as associated concerns.

Second Place ($1,000)

Thought4Food developed by Mary and Gregory Everhart (Kenmore); is a civic app that helps make it easier to donate food that cannot be sold or used. It helps those who donate, especially farmers, realize the potential tax credits available by calculating their potential credits based on the USDA commodity market value. This helps to demonstrate the value of donations by businesses or farmers and makes donation a more viable option.

Third Place ($500)

Buffalo Snow developed by Jesse Thomson (Buffalo), a civic app that p[provides real time locations for municipal snowplows and weather data for what roads are clear throughout Western New York to provide drivers knowledge on road conditions and making winter travel safer.

WNY Civic Hackathon Rules

  • Teams can be up to four members.
  • Participants must submit their applications based on one of the civic themes.
  • Unfortunately we won't be able to provide the participants with any hardware products. Participants are encouraged to bring their own in case they wish to incorporate the same in their hack.
  • There will be topic specific datasets available for use for the participants. Participants can choose to use these datasets or can use any other open source available datasets. If you wish to use data off any research you have previously done, please cite the source.
  • All teams retain full ownership of what they have created during the hackathon.
  • Be respectful. Be thoughtful. Be open. Be awesome and have fun!

Demos

  • All teams are required to submit their hack. At least one team member should be present in order to give a demo for their hack to be presented.
  • Demos of your hack include but are not restricted to: Live Demos, Presentations, Code.
  • Hack Submissions should be straightforward and easy to understand.
  • Mockups or anything already launched is strictly prohibited from submission. Any attempt of doing so can lead to immediate disqualification.
  • Time Limit:
    Each demo should be not more than 300 seconds (5 minutes). Presenters will be told ahead of time and will be told how much time remains during the presentation.
